Dostoevsky is the brooding, complex hubby of my literary dreams—mysterious, intense, and always pulling me into the deepest corners of the human soul. His novels are long, yes, but every page feels like a midnight conversation with someone who sees straight into your soul. He’s the kind of husband who challenges you constantly—philosophically, emotionally, spiritually—and just when you think you understand him, he throws in another plot twist or existential crisis. Whether it's the tormented Raskolnikov in Crime and Punishment, the chaotic brilliance of The Brothers Karamazov, or the heartbreak in Notes from Underground, Dostoevsky never lets my mind rest. He’s dark, dramatic, and demands my full attention—just the way I like it.
